Brand Direction Study · April 2026

Three ways to make Norr AI look like something

Right now Norr AI looks like every other SaaS tool — dark slate background, one accent blue, generic sans-serif. Below are three directions that actually commit to a point of view. Each uses the same name and the same tagline slot, but with different color, typography, and structural choices. Scroll through, see which one makes you feel something.

Direction A
Nordic
Almanac

Editorial, quietly confident, with Scandinavian restraint and just enough warmth to feel human. Thinks of itself as the trade journal of AI automation — substantive, crafted, built to be kept on a shelf. The "Norr" in Norr AI does real work here.

Direction A

Nordic Almanac

Strongest on the name. Best for owning the "quiet craft" narrative. Might feel a touch precious for insurance broker audiences — dentists and realtors will love it.

Editorial Warm Premium Scandinavian
Direction B

Polar Modern

Safest — reads as "serious tech company" without being generic SaaS. Scales best if you ever move beyond Faribault. Less distinctive than A or C, but hardest to get wrong.

Minimal Scalable Architectural Premium
Direction C

Midwestern Infrastructure

Most distinctive in the AI automation category — zero competitors look like this. Best match for your actual background (data engineer, builds pipes). Harder to pull off if you ever hire designers who haven't seen the vision.
