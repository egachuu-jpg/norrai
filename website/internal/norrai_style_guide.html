06 · Voice & Tone

How Norr AI sounds

The visual system is only half the brand. Copy needs to match it — precise, confident, never breathless. ROI-first always.

01
Lead with the number
Dentists think in appointment values. Realtors think in GCI. Insurance brokers think in retained premium. Start there, not with "AI" or "automation".
02
Confidence, not hype
Never "game-changing", "revolutionary", or "cutting-edge". The work speaks. The copy stays calm. Precision over superlatives.
03
Short sentences win
The logo is two words. The tagline is four. Keep the copy tight. One idea per sentence. Cut the sentence if removing it changes nothing.
✓ Write this
"One extra closed deal pays for the Starter tier many times over."
Concrete, ROI-first, no jargon. The client can do the math themselves.
✗ Not this
"Our cutting-edge AI-powered automation platform revolutionizes the way real estate professionals engage with leads."
Every word here is a red flag. Starts with "our", loaded with buzzwords, zero specifics.
✓ Write this
"Every missed call gets a text back before the caller's car pulls out of your lot."
Specific, visual, makes the benefit tangible without explaining the technology behind it.
✗ Not this
"Leverage our intelligent n8n workflows and Claude API integration to streamline your lead response processes."
Never name the stack in client copy. They don't care. The outcome is the pitch.
✓ Write this
"If there were clients about to leave at renewal and you didn't know who — what would it be worth to find out in advance?"
Question format that sells itself. Makes the problem vivid before offering the solution.
✗ Not this
"We are excited to offer a best-in-class solution that helps your business grow and scale to new heights!"
Excited is never the right energy. Exclamation points are banned. "Best-in-class" means nothing.
Punctuation & formatting rules
Em dashes — yes
Use em dashes freely. They're punchy and precise. No spaces around them.
Exclamation points — never
Not in copy, not in emails. The work is impressive enough without them.
Oxford comma — always
Dentists, realtors, and insurance brokers. Not dentists, realtors and insurance brokers.
Numbers — numerals
Always use numerals for numbers (5 minutes, not five minutes). Numbers are data.
Headers — sentence case
Sentence case for all headings. Title Case Looks Bureaucratic. don't do it.
Arrows → yes
→ for flow, sequence, and process. It's in the DNA of the brand. Use it in UI copy where it fits.
