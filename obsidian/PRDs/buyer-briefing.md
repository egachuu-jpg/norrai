# PRD: Buyer Briefing Generator

**Status:** Draft
**Author:** Egan
**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Problem Statement

Agents schedule showings but buyers often walk in cold — no context on the school district, no sense of how the price compares to recent sales, no feel for market conditions. The agent has to either brief them verbally (takes time, easy to forget) or the buyer Googles on the drive over and asks questions the agent wasn't prepared for.

A one-page pre-showing briefing document — sent automatically when a showing is scheduled — gives buyers the context they need and makes the agent look prepared without adding manual work.

---

## Goals

- Generate a concise buyer briefing document in under 60 seconds from a property address
- Deliver it to the buyer via email before the showing
- Cover: market conditions, nearby schools, walkability, informal comp context, and what to look for at this price point
- Require zero agent effort beyond entering the address and buyer email
- Stay within fair housing compliance guardrails

## Non-Goals

- Replace a formal CMA — this is market context, not a valuation
- Provide interior photos or listing copy — MLS handles that
- Automate showing scheduling — this is triggered by the agent manually
- Serve as legal advice on pricing or offer strategy

---

## Users

**Primary:** Real estate agents on Norr AI Starter or Growth tier
**Indirect beneficiaries:** Buyers who receive the briefing and arrive more prepared

**Use cases:**
- First showing on a property the buyer found online
- Showing a buyer a property the agent found for them
- Open house follow-up where the attendee expressed continued interest

## Input Contract

```json
{
  "agent_name": "Alex Rivera",
  "agent_email": "alex@example.com",
  "buyer_name": "Sarah Johnson",
  "buyer_email": "sarah@gmail.com",
  "address": "198 Cuylle Ct",
  "city": "Lakeville",
  "state": "MN",
  "zip": "55044",
  "beds": 4,
  "baths": 2,
  "price_range": "$280k-$320k",
  "showing_datetime": "2026-05-14T14:00:00",
  "agent_notes": "New roof 2022, updated kitchen. Owner is motivated."
}
```

`agent_notes` is optional — if provided, Claude can reference it to prepare the buyer without over-sharing seller motivation.

## Output: Buyer Briefing Email

Sent from `hello@norrai.co`, From Name set to agent's name, Reply-To set to agent's email.

**Subject:** Your briefing for 198 Cuylle Ct — Wednesday at 2pm

**Email structure:**
1. **The market right now** — median DOM, inventory level, YoY trend (1 paragraph)
2. **How this home compares** — informal comp context, where the asking price sits vs. recent sales (1 paragraph, hedged)
3. **Schools nearby** — list of 2-3 public schools with grades and GreatSchools rating
4. **Getting around** — walkability description (1-2 sentences)
5. **Questions worth asking** — 3-4 neutral, practical questions a buyer should ask at this showing (Claude-generated based on property type and price point)
6. Footer: agent name, reply-to email, `comps_disclaimer`

**Tone:** Warm, practical, buyer-friendly. Written as if the agent prepared it personally, not generated by a system.

---

## Claude Prompt Structure

```
You are writing a pre-showing briefing for a real estate buyer. Write in a warm, 
practical tone as if the agent prepared it personally.

PROPERTY: [address], [city], [state] [zip]
SHOWING: [showing_datetime]
AGENT: [agent_name]
BUYER: [buyer_name]
BEDS/BATHS: [beds]/[baths]
PRICE RANGE: [price_range]
AGENT NOTES: [agent_notes or "none"]

MARKET DATA:
[insight_block from research agent]

RECENT COMPS:
[recent_comps from research agent]

Rules:
- Only use facts from the research data above. Do not invent statistics or school names.
- Never mention neighborhood demographics, crime, or any protected class characteristics.
- Present comp data as "based on publicly available sales data" — never as a precise valuation.
- The "Questions worth asking" section should be practical and neutral — not leading.
- Keep the entire email under 350 words.
- If research data confidence is low for a category, omit that section rather than hedging heavily.
```

## Agent-Facing UI: clients/buyer_briefing.html

A form behind Cloudflare Access (clients group).

**Form fields:**
- Buyer name
- Buyer email
- Property address (street, city, state, zip)
- Beds / Baths
- Asking price or price range
- Showing date and time (datetime-local input)
- Agent notes (optional textarea, placeholder: "anything you want the buyer to know or questions they should ask")

**Success state:** "Briefing sent to [buyer_email]. They should have it within 60 seconds."

**localStorage:** Save agent name and agent email across sessions (same pattern as listing_form.html).

## Fair Housing Compliance

Same guardrails as the research agent:
- No demographics, no crime stats, no protected-class language
- School data presented as public record fact, not as a neighborhood quality signal
- Comp data always hedged with `comps_disclaimer`
- Claude prompt explicitly prohibits steering language

---

## Neon Logging

Log `triggered` and `completed` events to `workflow_events` with:

## Open Questions

1. **Reply-to address** — should the email come From the agent's name with Reply-To the agent's email, or from `hello@norrai.co` with the agent's contact in the footer? The former feels more personal; the latter is simpler from a SendGrid sender reputation standpoint.
2. **Showing confirmation integration** — longer term, could trigger automatically when a Calendly/Acuity showing is booked rather than requiring agent form submission.
3. **Low research confidence** — if research agent returns `data_confidence.comps: low` for a rural property, should the email omit the comp section entirely, or include it with a heavy caveat?
