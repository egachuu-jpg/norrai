# Insurance Playbook

## Pitch

**Lead with:** renewal math.

"If you have 500 policies at an average premium of $2,000, that's $1M in book. If you lose 8% at renewal — industry average — that's $80,000 in premium walking out the door every year. We build you an early warning system that flags which clients are at risk 90 days out."

**Key qualifying question:** "If I told you there were clients about to leave at renewal and you don't know who they are — what would it be worth to find out in advance?"

**Never say:** "automation," "n8n," "Claude."

**Salesforce positioning:** "We complete Salesforce, not compete with it."

---

## Pain points to listen for

- "Renewals sneak up on me"
- "I lose clients I didn't know were unhappy"
- "I spend too much time on manual follow-up"
- "I'm not cross-selling as much as I should"

---

## Qualifying questions

- How many policies in your book?
- What's your average premium per policy?
- What's your retention rate at renewal?
- What CRM are you on? (Salesforce = good; EZLynx, Applied Epic = ask)
- Do you have support staff or are you solo?

---

## ROI math

_Book size × avg premium × churn rate = at-risk premium_

500 policies × $2,000 × 8% = $80,000/yr at risk.
Recovering 25% of that = $20,000/yr in retained premium.
Starter at $600/mo = $7,200/yr. Easy math.

---

## Gotchas

- Salesforce integration is powerful but complex — understand their data model before promising anything
- EZLynx and Applied Epic have APIs but limited documentation — research before the proposal
- E&O concerns: don't let the AI give coverage advice; keep it to logistics (reminders, follow-up, scheduling)
- Insurance broker friend is the first target — discovery call framework ready
